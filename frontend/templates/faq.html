Features

What is NDVI analysis and how does it work?

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) is a measure of vegetation health using satellite imagery.

How it works: Compares near-infrared and visible light reflectance from plants
Scale: Values from -1 to +1, where higher values indicate healthier vegetation
Applications: Crop health monitoring, yield prediction, irrigation planning
Data source: Sentinel-2 satellite imagery updated every 5 days

Our platform automatically processes this data to create easy-to-understand heatmaps and trends for your fields.

How accurate are the yield predictions?

Our AI models achieve high accuracy rates:

  • Corn: 92-96% accuracy within 10% of actual yield
  • Soybeans: 89-94% accuracy within 10% of actual yield
  • Wheat: 87-92% accuracy within 10% of actual yield

Accuracy depends on several factors:

  • Quality and completeness of input data
  • Historical data availability for your region
  • Weather conditions and unexpected events
  • Time in growing season (later predictions are more accurate)

Data & Analysis

Where does the satellite data come from?

We use multiple high-quality satellite data sources:

  • Sentinel-2: Primary source for NDVI and multispectral analysis (10m resolution, 5-day revisit)
  • Landsat 8/9: Long-term historical data (30m resolution, 16-day revisit)
  • Planet Labs: High-resolution daily imagery for premium users (3m resolution)

All data is processed through cloud computing systems to remove atmospheric interference and ensure accuracy.

How is my data protected and secured?

We take data security very seriously:

Encryption: All data encrypted in transit (TLS 1.3) and at rest (AES-256)
Access Control: Role-based permissions and multi-factor authentication
Compliance: SOC 2 Type II certified, GDPR compliant
Privacy: Your field data is private and never shared without consent
Backups: Regular automated backups with disaster recovery procedures

Researchers only access aggregated, anonymized data for scientific purposes.
